About West Bengal


West Bengal (/bɛnˈɡɔːl/, Bengali: Poshchim Bongo, pronounced [ˈpoʃtʃim ˈbɔŋɡo] ⓘ, abbr. WB) is a state in the eastern portion of India. It is situated along the Bay of Bengal, along with a population of over 91 million inhabitants within an area of 88,752 km2 (34,267 sq mi) as of 2011. The population estimate as of 2023 is 102,552,787.[12] West Bengal is the fourth-most populous and thirteenth-largest state by area in India, as well as the eighth-most populous country subdivision of the world. As a part of the Bengal region of the Indian subcontinent, it borders Bangladesh in the east, and Nepal and Bhutan in the north. It also borders the Indian states of Jharkhand, Odisha, Bihar, Sikkim and Assam. The state capital is Kolkata, the third-largest metropolis, and seventh largest city by population in India. West Bengal includes the Darjeeling Himalayan hill region, the Ganges delta, the Rarh region, the coastal Sundarbans and the Bay of Bengal. The state's main ethnic group are the Bengalis, with the Bengali Hindus forming the demographic majority.


The origin of the name Bengal (Bangla and Bongo in Bengali) is unknown. One theory suggests the word derives from "Bang", the name of a Dravidian tribe that settled the region around 1000 BCE.[30] The Bengali word Bongo might have been derived from the ancient kingdom of Vanga (or Banga). Although some early Sanskrit literature mentions the name Vanga, the region's early history is obscure.[31].


Rice and fish are traditional favourite foods, leading to a saying in Bengali, "machhe bhate bangali", that translates as "fish and rice make a Bengali".[202] Bengal's vast repertoire of fish-based dishes includes hilsa preparations, a favourite among Bengalis. There are numerous ways of cooking fish depending on its texture, size, fat content and bones.[203] Most of the people also consume eggs, chicken, mutton, and shrimp. Panta bhat (rice soaked overnight in water) with onion and green chili is a traditional dish consumed in rural areas.[204] Common spices found in a Bengali kitchen include cumin, ajmoda (radhuni), bay leaf, mustard, ginger, green chillies and turmeric.[205] Sweets occupy an important place in the diet of Bengalis and at their social ceremonies. Bengalis make distinctive sweetmeats from milk products, including Rôshogolla, Chômchôm, Kalojam and several kinds of sondesh. Pitha, a kind of sweet cake, bread, or dim sum, are specialties of the winter season. Sweets such as narkol-naru, til-naru, moa and payesh are prepared during festivals such as Lakshmi puja.[206] Popular street foods include Aloor Chop, Beguni, Kati roll, biryani, and phuchka.[207][208]

Best Time to Visit in Sikkim


Summer in west bengal

Travelling in West Bengal during the summer offers a mix of challenges and delights. While the plains can be sweltering with temperatures soaring, especially in urban areas like Kolkata, the hill stations provide a cool escape with refreshing mountain air. Exploring destinations like Darjeeling, Kalimpong, and Mirik offers a respite from the heat, allowing visitors to enjoy lush greenery and scenic vistas.


Monsoon in west Bengal

Travelling in West Bengal during the monsoon season offers a unique and immersive experience. While the rainfall rejuvenates the lush green landscapes, especially in regions like the Sundarbans and Darjeeling, it can also lead to occasional disruptions in transportation and outdoor activities. However, the monsoon brings out the state's natural beauty in full force, making it an ideal time for exploring verdant forests, witnessing cascading waterfalls, and enjoying the cooler weather in the hills.


Winter in Sikkim The

Travelling in West Bengal during winter offers a delightful experience characterized by pleasant weather and vibrant cultural festivities. With temperatures ranging from comfortable to mildly cool, especially in the hill stations like Darjeeling and Kalimpong, it's an ideal time for sightseeing, outdoor activities, and exploring the rich heritage of places like Kolkata and Shantiniketan. The season also hosts iconic festivals like Durga Puja, adding to the vibrant atmosphere. Overall, winter in West Bengal promises a memorable blend of cultural immersion and scenic beauty

How to Reach Sikkim


Diverse and enchanting, West Bengal welcomes visitors with its rich cultural heritage, stunning landscapes, and vibrant cities. Whether you're traveling by air, road, or rail, here are the main transportation options to reach this captivating state:


By Air:

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata, Bagdogra Airport near Siliguri, and Durgapur Airport serve West Bengal. Taxis and buses provide connectivity to various destinations within the state


By Road:

West Bengal has a well-developed road network, with inter-city buses, taxis, and rental cars connecting major towns and cities. The state is also well-connected to neighboring states


By Rail:

West Bengal's extensive railway network connects major cities and towns, with Howrah Junction and Sealdah Station in Kolkata serving as major hubs. Other important stations include New Jalpaiguri, Asansol, and Durgapur

Places to visit in West Bengal


Kolkata: The Cultural Capital!

**District headquarters:** Kolkata
Starting with Kolkata, the capital city of West Bengal, immerse yourself in its rich cultural heritage, bustling streets, and colonial architecture. Explore iconic landmarks such as the Victoria Memorial, a majestic marble structure that houses a museum showcasing the history of British India, and Howrah Bridge, one of the busiest cantilever bridges in the world, offering panoramic views of the Hooghly River. Visit Dakshineswar Kali Temple, a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kali, and experience the spiritual ambiance of the place amidst devotees and rituals. Indulge in the city's culinary delights, from savory street food like phuchka and kathi rolls to sumptuous Bengali thalis featuring dishes like fish curry, roshogolla, and mishti doi. Kolkata's vibrant arts and music scene beckon with theaters, galleries, and live performances showcasing the city's cultural diversity. Don't miss the colorful festivities during Durga Puja, Kolkata's biggest celebration, when the city comes alive with pandal hopping, cultural programs, and elaborate decorations honoring the goddess Durga.


Darjeeling: The Queen of Hills!

District headquarters: Darjeeling
Journey to Darjeeling, renowned for its tea gardens, stunning views of the Himalayas, and the iconic Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Explore the sprawling tea estates where the world-famous Darjeeling tea is cultivated, and immerse yourself in the serene ambiance amidst lush greenery. Visit the Tiger Hill at sunrise for breathtaking panoramic views of the snow-capped peaks of Kanchenjunga and the surrounding Himalayan range, a sight that will leave you spellbound. Explore the vibrant markets of Darjeeling, bustling with activity and offering an array of handicrafts, souvenirs, and local delicacies. Sample the region's famous tea at one of the many tea houses and learn about the intricate process of tea production. Discover the peace and serenity of Buddhist monasteries like Ghoom Monastery, home to a magnificent statue of Maitreya Buddha and ancient Tibetan manuscripts. Engage in adventurous activities like trekking to Sandakphu, the highest peak in West Bengal, or mountain biking through the scenic trails amidst the breathtaking landscapes of Darjeeling. Whether you seek tranquility or adventure, Darjeeling offers an unforgettable experience for every traveler.


Sundarbans: Where Nature Meets Adventure!

**District headquarters:** Gosaba
Explore the Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Cruise through its winding waterways, home to the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger and diverse wildlife including crocodiles, deer, and exotic birds. Experience the unique culture of the local communities, known for their traditional way of life and folklore. Discover the ecological wonders of this biodiverse region through guided tours and eco-friendly adventures.


Shantiniketan: Abode of Peace and Art!

**District headquarters:** Bolpur
Visit Shantiniketan, the tranquil abode founded by Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore. Immerse yourself in the artistic ambiance of Visva-Bharati University, Tagore's home, and the vibrant cultural festivals like Poush Mela and Basanta Utsav. Explore the serene surroundings, lush gardens, and museums showcasing Tagore's works. Engage in creative pursuits like music, dance, and handicrafts, and rejuvenate your soul amidst nature's beauty.

Siliguri: Gateway to the North-East!

**District headquarters:** Siliguri Siliguri serves as the gateway to the northeastern states and offers a blend of urban convenience and natural beauty. Explore the Mahananda Wildlife Sanctuary, home to diverse flora and fauna, or embark on a scenic drive to Mirik, known for its serene lakes and tea gardens. Visit the ISKCON temple or enjoy shopping at Hong Kong Market for local handicrafts and souvenirs. Siliguri is also a convenient base for exploring nearby destinations like Darjeeling and Sikkim.

Bankura: Land of Temples and Terracotta!

**District headquarters:** Bankura Discover the architectural marvels of Bankura, known for its terracotta temples and rich heritage. Explore the ancient temples of Bishnupur, adorned with intricate terracotta carvings depicting scenes from Hindu mythology. Visit the Susunia Hills for trekking and rock climbing adventures amidst scenic landscapes and ancient cave paintings. Experience the vibrant culture of the region through traditional music, dance, and handicrafts.

Things to do in West Bengal


From the bustling streets of Kolkata to the tranquil hills of Darjeeling, West Bengal offers a myriad of activities to enchant travelers. Adventure seekers can embark on thrilling treks amidst the lush greenery of the Darjeeling hills or explore the rugged terrain of the Sundarbans on a thrilling jungle safari. Rafting enthusiasts can conquer the rapids of the Teesta River, while paragliding enthusiasts can soar high above the scenic landscapes of Kalimpong. Jeep safaris through the dense forests of Dooars provide an opportunity to spot rare wildlife species like the Bengal tiger and Indian rhinoceros in their natural habitat.


Wildlife enthusiasts can visit the famous Sunderbans National Park, home to the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger and a diverse array of flora and fauna. Experience the unique thrill of riding a yak through the picturesque valleys of Darjeeling or take a cable car ride to witness panoramic views of the city skyline. Birdwatchers can explore the mangrove forests of the Sundarbans or the lush tea gardens of Dooars to spot a variety of avian species including the elusive red panda and colorful peacocks. Don't miss the chance to savor the aromatic flavors of Darjeeling tea at one of the region's iconic tea estates or indulge in a shopping spree at Kolkata's vibrant markets, where you can find exquisite handicrafts, traditional sarees, and delectable street food delights.
