About WestBengal


Assam (/əˈsæm/, Assamese: অসম, pronounced [ɔxɔm]) is a state in northeastern India, known for its rich biodiversity and cultural heritage. It is bordered by Bhutan and Arunachal Pradesh to the north; Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram, and Tripura to the east; Meghalaya, Bangladesh, and West Bengal to the south; and the Indian states of Sikkim and Bihar to the west. With a population of over 35 million inhabitants and an area of 78,438 km2 (30,285 sq mi) as of 2011, Assam is the most populous and the sixteenth-largest state in terms of area in India.


The etymology of the name "Assam" is uncertain. Some suggest it is derived from the Ahom dynasty which ruled the region for several centuries. Others propose it may have originated from the Sanskrit word "Asoma" meaning "peerless" or "unequaled." Assam has a diverse population comprising various ethnic groups including Assamese, Bodo, Bengali, and various indigenous tribes.


Assam boasts a vibrant culinary culture, with rice as a staple food like much of the Indian subcontinent. Traditional Assamese cuisine features a variety of dishes made with fish, meat, and vegetables. Some popular dishes include Masor Tenga (sour fish curry), Duck Roast, Omita Khar (raw papaya curry), and Aloo Pitika (mashed potato with mustard oil and spices). Assam is also known for its rich tea gardens, producing some of the finest tea in the world.


Festivals play a significant role in Assamese culture. Bihu, celebrated three times a year (Rongali Bihu, Bhogali Bihu, and Kongali Bihu), is the most important festival, marking the Assamese New Year and the agricultural cycle. Other festivals include Durga Puja, Diwali, and Magh Bihu. Traditional Assamese attire includes the Mekhela Chador for women and the Gamosa for both men and women, which hold cultural significance and are often worn during festivals and ceremonies.

Best Time to Visit in Assam


Summer in Assam

Travelling in Assam during the summer offers a diverse range of experiences. While the Brahmaputra Valley experiences hot and humid weather, the hill stations such as Haflong and Dima Hasao offer cooler temperatures, making them perfect retreats from the heat. Exploring the lush tea gardens, wildlife sanctuaries like Kaziranga, and cultural landmarks such as Kamakhya Temple can be enjoyable during this time, despite the warm weather.


Monsoon in Assam

Travelling in Assam during the monsoon season brings out the state's natural beauty in its full glory. The rainfall transforms the landscape into a verdant paradise, rejuvenating the tea gardens, forests, and wildlife sanctuaries. However, heavy rains may lead to occasional floods and disruptions in transportation and outdoor activities. Despite these challenges, witnessing the lush greenery, flowing rivers, and cascading waterfalls during the monsoon can be a truly immersive experience for visitors.


Winter in Assam

Travelling in Assam during winter offers pleasant weather and a chance to explore the state's cultural heritage and natural wonders. With temperatures ranging from cool to mild, it's an ideal time for outdoor activities like wildlife safaris in Kaziranga National Park and exploring historical sites such as Sivasagar and Majuli Island. The winter season also hosts festivals like Bihu, showcasing Assamese culture and traditions. Overall, winter in Assam promises a delightful blend of cultural exploration and natural beauty.

How to Reach Assam


With its breathtaking landscapes and vibrant culture, Assam offers a warm welcome to visitors from all corners of the globe. Here are the main transportation options to reach this enchanting state:


By Air:

Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport in Guwahati, Dibrugarh Airport, and Silchar Airport serve Assam, offering domestic and limited international flights. From the airports, taxis, and buses are readily available for onward travel to various destinations within the state.


By Road:

Assam boasts a well-connected road network, with state and national highways linking major cities and towns. Inter-city buses, taxis, and rental cars provide convenient transportation options for travelers. The state is also well-connected to neighboring states such as Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, and Nagaland.


By Rail:

Assam's extensive railway network ensures easy access to the state. Guwahati Railway Station, the largest railway station in Assam, serves as a major transportation hub. Other important railway stations include Dibrugarh, Silchar, and Jorhat. Regular train services connect Assam to various parts of the country, facilitating smooth travel for visitors.

Places to visit in Assam


Guwahati: Gateway to the Northeast!

**District headquarters:** Kamrup Metropolitan
Begin your journey in Guwahati, the bustling gateway to Northeast India. Explore the Kamakhya Temple, a revered Hindu pilgrimage site dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya, situated atop Nilachal Hill. Take a serene boat ride on the Brahmaputra River during sunset and witness the breathtaking views of the cityscape and surrounding hills. Visit the Assam State Museum to delve into the rich cultural heritage of Assam, showcasing archaeological artifacts, sculptures, and tribal crafts. Indulge in the vibrant markets of Fancy Bazar and Paltan Bazar, offering a variety of local handicrafts, silk sarees, and Assamese tea. Guwahati also serves as a convenient base for exploring nearby attractions such as Kaziranga National Park and Pobitora Wildlife Sanctuary.


Kaziranga National Park: A Wildlife Wonderland!

**District headquarters:** Golaghat
Embark on a thrilling safari adventure in Kaziranga National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to the one-horned rhinoceros. Explore the vast grasslands, marshes, and dense forests teeming with diverse wildlife including tigers, elephants, and wild water buffalo. Experience the unique ecosystem of the park through jeep safaris, elephant rides, and birdwatching tours. Immerse yourself in the natural beauty of Kaziranga and witness the conservation efforts aimed at preserving this biodiversity hotspot.


Majuli Island: A Cultural Oasis!

**District headquarters:** Jorhat
Discover the cultural heritage of Majuli Island, the world's largest river island located amidst the Brahmaputra River. Explore the ancient Vaishnavite monasteries known as "Satras," which serve as centers for Assamese culture, music, and dance. Witness traditional performances like Sattriya dance and Borgeet, a form of devotional music, showcasing the rich cultural traditions of Assam. Engage in pottery making, mask crafting, and weaving workshops, and interact with the local Mishing and Deori communities to learn about their way of life. Majuli offers a serene retreat amidst scenic landscapes and tranquil villages, making it a haven for cultural enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.


Sibsagar: Legacy of the Ahom Dynasty!

**District headquarters:** Sivasagar
Explore the historical landmarks of Sibsagar, the ancient capital of the Ahom Kingdom and a treasure trove of Assamese heritage. Visit the majestic Sivasagar Sivadol, a towering temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, adorned with intricate carvings and architectural grandeur. Explore the Rang Ghar, a centuries-old amphitheater used for royal ceremonies and cultural performances during the Ahom rule. Discover the ancient Ahom palaces, reservoirs, and temples scattered across the town, reflecting the architectural prowess and cultural legacy of the Ahom dynasty. Sibsagar offers a glimpse into Assam's royal past and architectural marvels, making it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.

Things to do in West Bengal


From the bustling streets of Kolkata to the tranquil hills of Darjeeling, West Bengal offers a myriad of activities to enchant travelers. Adventure seekers can embark on thrilling treks amidst the lush greenery of the Darjeeling hills or explore the rugged terrain of the Sundarbans on a thrilling jungle safari. Rafting enthusiasts can conquer the rapids of the Teesta River, while paragliding enthusiasts can soar high above the scenic landscapes of Kalimpong. Jeep safaris through the dense forests of Dooars provide an opportunity to spot rare wildlife species like the Bengal tiger and Indian rhinoceros in their natural habitat.


Wildlife enthusiasts can visit the famous Sunderbans National Park, home to the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger and a diverse array of flora and fauna. Experience the unique thrill of riding a yak through the picturesque valleys of Darjeeling or take a cable car ride to witness panoramic views of the city skyline. Birdwatchers can explore the mangrove forests of the Sundarbans or the lush tea gardens of Dooars to spot a variety of avian species including the elusive red panda and colorful peacocks. Don't miss the chance to savor the aromatic flavors of Darjeeling tea at one of the region's iconic tea estates or indulge in a shopping spree at Kolkata's vibrant markets, where you can find exquisite handicrafts, traditional sarees, and delectable street food delights.
