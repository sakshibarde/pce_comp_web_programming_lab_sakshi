Best Time to Visit in Kerala


Summer in Kerala

Summer season in Kerala commences from the month of March and continues till May. During this, Kerala remains hot and humid with intermittent rainfalls. However, the evenings are quite pleasant and one can think of planning a visit in summer in Kerala. In fact summer is a good time to enjoy beach activities at the Thiruvananthapuram, Kovalam, Varkala and sightseeing at Alleppey, Wayanad and Munnar. Summer is also the season of celebrating elephant festivals like ThrissurPooram, which takes place in late April or early May each year.


Monsoon in Kerala

Monsoon season in Kerala begins from month of June and last till September. Expect heavy rains, which by the enhance the beauty of the entire state making it more green and alluring. Monsoon is an ideal time for Ayurvedic treatments, it is believed that Ayurveda therapies work best in the rainy season as the pores of the skin opens during this season. Also, it is a perfect time to enjoy sightseeing at different destinations like Idukki, Kovalam and Wayanad.


Winter in Kerala The

October marks the onset of winter season in Kerala that continues till the month of March. During this season, the weather remains pleasant and ideal for planning a perfect holiday in Kerala. It is in fact the best time to explore every part of the state, right from hill stations to the beach getaways or the backwaters. Winter season is also ideal for a visit particularly to Kottayam, Alleppey, Varkala, Kozhikode and Thekkady.

How to Reach Kerala


Kerala is a small state located in the south-western part of India. Kerala is edged by a 580 km long coastline. Towards its west, there is the Arabian Sea and towards its east, there is the Eastern Ghats, which act as a differentiation point between Tamil Nadu and Kerala.


By Air:

There are three airports in the state: Thiruvananthapuram International Airport, 10 km from Thiruvananthapuram (Southern Part of Kerala)Cochin International Airport, Ernakulam (Central Park of Kerala)Karipur or Calicut International Airport, 27 km from Kozhikode (Northern Part of Kerala) There are 3 more airports which are quite close to Kerala: Mangalore International AirportCoimbatore International AirportMadurai Airport Kerala is accessible by air from most parts of India, especially from South-Eastern towns, namely Bangalore, Chennai and Hyderabad.


By Road:

There are three major highways which connect Kerala with the rest of India i.e. National Highways 17, 47 and 49. These three highways are directly connected with Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.


By Rail:

There are 200 railway stations which connect Kerala with the rest of India. Every district and town of Kerala can be reached by railways other than its hill stations, Wayanad, Munnar and Thekkady. Railways services also link the state with many metropolitan cities like New Delhi, Kolkata and Chennai.

Places to visit in Kerala


Munnar

If you have ever seen romanticised scenes in Bollywood songs set on a hill trail covered with green tea meadows, then you know what Munnar looks like. This hill station is widely famous for enchanting experiences amidst tea gardens and mesmerising sunrises and sunsets. Additionally, Munnar is a hotspot for wildlife and forest lovers, as the hill station has several wildlife sanctuaries and national parks in and around it. Along with uncommon species of animals like the Tiger, Leopard, and Sambar deer, Munnar is one of the few locations to witness the blue fields of Neelakurinji, a majestic and rare flower species that predominantly grows in the Western Ghats. If you are looking for a trip where you can be in the midst of nature, then this is the best place to be in Kerala.
Best Time to Visit : Throughout the year. Top Places to Visit : Blossom Park, Attukal Waterfalls, Kundala Lake, Pothamedu Viewpoint, Marayoor Dolmens, Eravikulam National Park and Cheeyappara Waterfalls.


Alleppe

Alleppey is one of the most scenic and famous locations in Kerala. As the pristine backwaters and boat pictures could be seen ubiquitously depicting the state, it would not be wrong to say it is the poster city of Kerala. It was also named the "Venice of the East" by Lord Curzon during the English colonisation period. Massively known for the Snake boat race or officially called Nehru Trophy Boat Race, brings people in massive figures to Kayamkulam Lake. Moreover, from hosting the backwater races, Kerala also attracts tourists to enjoy the houseboat rides and other water adventure activities here. Furthermore, Kerala is also a birdwatching paradise for nature enthusiasts, especially the Pathiramanal island. Also, the fascinating experience of shopping at Canal Bazaar is one experience that everyone loves. Best Time to Visit : August to March. Top Places to Visit : Alappuzha Beach, Marari Beach, Punnamada Lake, Revikarnukaran Museum.


Thekkady

Thekkady is a plantation town mostly famous for its rich natural surroundings, wildlife, and spices. This place is a heartland of spies, and you can find a lot of spices being farmed. Whatsoever, the town is comprehensively known for the Periyar wildlife region. A considerable area of Thekkady is encapsulated by Periyar National Park. There is a lot you can do in the national park itself. Also, the park is a tiger reserve; however, it has a very thin chance of encountering tigers due to their very low density. The only tiger reserve you can take a walk on, Periyar tiger reserve has walking trails inside the tiger reserve but only under the guidance of experts. Walking, trekking, and encountering wild animals in the park is one of the best things to do in Thekkady. Periyar Lake, which comes under the area of the park, also boasts a pristine surrounding that is loved by many. Along with that, you can take a boat ride or do rafting here. Above all, most of the perks go to the indigenous tribes that live around as the handlers in the park are people from the tribe. You can also visit the tribal areas, watch their ways, see the tribal dance, or dance with them. Pun intended! Top Places to Visit : Periyar National Park, Elephant Junction, Periyar Lake, and Spice Walk. Best Time to Visit : Between September and March are the most pleasant months.


Kochi (Cochin)

Located in the heart of Kerala, Cochin stands as a quintessential destination, offering an immersive experience of the state's essence. Serving as the ideal starting point for a Kerala exploration, Cochin encapsulates the captivating blend of tradition and modernity. Beyond its familiar name, the encompassing Ernakulam is a treasure trove along the Arabian Sea. A city that seamlessly marries contemporary vigour with ancient elegance, Cochin showcases a cultural tapestry woven by British, Dutch, and Portuguese influences. Initially a trading hub, Ernakulam transformed into the bustling commercial capital of Kerala, adorned by remnants of colonial encounters. Known as the 'Queen of the Arabian Sea,' Cochin's harbour remains a testament to its historical significance, attracting diverse cultures including the British, Dutch, Arabs, Portuguese, and Chinese, leaving an indelible mark on its landscape. Best Time to Visit : May to March.
